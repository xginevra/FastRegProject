What you need to know about EHRs

EHR stands for Electronic Health Record.
It is a digital version of a patient's paper chart.
It contains comprehensive and real-time information about a patient's
medical records.
EHRs are designed improve communication between healthcare
providers, and enhance the overall quality of patient care.
They can be accessed securely by authorized healthcare professionals
and are a crucial component of modern healthcare systems.
